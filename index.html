Research Paper and Publications

Strategic Policy Interventions for India’s $5T Economy

Year 2025

The recent Global Investors Summit 2025, organized in Bhopal on February 24th and 25th, saw the launch of 18 policies to boost investment, industrial development, and economic growth in India, specifically in Madhya Pradesh. With the motto "Why spend more when you can have more?", the summit focused on cost-efficient but high-return investment options. This research paper fits India's $5 trillion economy vision by 2027. By examining sectoral growth in manufacturing, finance, technology, and infrastructure, this research assesses the role of potential from these recently announced policies to attract foreign direct investment (FDI), provide jobs, and thereby drive GDP growth. In addition, this paper critically analyzes the policy implementation challenges and discusses strategic interventions to maximize returns while guaranteeing sustainable economic advancement. Through a rigorous evaluation, it shows important drivers that shape investment inflows, regulatory competence, and ease of doing business in general. The research also takes into consideration global economic trends and competitive positioning to offer insights into bettering India's investment environment. This study offers an integrated perspective on how the Global Investors Summit 2025 policies could shape India's long-term economic journey. It emphasizes the key role played by public- private collaborations, digitalization, and infrastructure construction in creating a business- friendly ecosystem. Furthermore, this paper emphasizes policy flexibility and governance frameworks to deliver maximum economic returns and sustainable growth. (Published)

Using data driven approaches to overcome barriers in Interdisciplinary Education

Year 2025

The rapid evolution of job markets needs continuous skill development to ensure that individuals stay relevant and adaptable in the competitive dynamic market. In the era of Artificial Intelligence, transformative fields have significant opportunities for learners from different academic backgrounds. However, non-technical students face psychological barriers which hinders their ability to learn these high demand skills. This study explores these barriers qualitatively, and identifies challenges faced by non-technical learners and the strategies to overcome them. The study conducted through a questionnaire filled out by 58 respondents finds that many students successfully overcome these obstacles by leveraging AI tools and seeking mentorship that simplified complex concepts and boosted their confidence. Upon these insights, this research paper proposes the use of data driven approaches to address psychological barriers in interdisciplinary education. The creation of customized learning programs, and tools designed to help students evaluate potential outcomes and benefits if they transit in this with their current educational background could play a major role in motivating continuous skill development and make the technical field more accessible. By addressing these psychological and practical challenges faced by non-technical learners. This innovative approach will help break the hurdle and will make people work confidently in any dynamic landscape of working professionals. (Published)

AI-Driven Circular Supply Chains

Year 2025

The increasing use of Artificial Intelligence in business processes has paved the way for sustainable and regenerative economic models. This study examines how AI-based demand forecasting can transform supply chains into circular systems eventually avoiding overproduction, minimizing waste, and facilitating just-in-time marketing strategies. Based on real data of Brazilian e-commerce company called Olist, the research demonstrates that by accurate demand forecasting it leads to optimum inventory and sustainable marketing efforts. This study supports the claim that AI has an immense capability for business expansion towards environmental sustenance, earning the status of an indispensable element of regenerative economics. Additionally, this study extends the economic and social implications of AI-powered circular supply chain, directing for efficient policies, best practices for industry and future developments.

CBDCs & Financial Inclusion in Emerging Economies

Year 2025

CBDCs are revolutionizing financial inclusion by enabling seamless digital transactions, reducing costs, and providing financial access to the unbanked population. In easy terms CBDC is like digital cash, you do not need a bank account to store unlike UPI, it gets stored directly in a digital wallet. It is just like holding physical cash but ONLINE! Countries like China, Nigeria, India, and Jamaica are already experimenting with CBDCs to facilitate financial inclusion, but challenges like cybersecurity, public trust, and digital infrastructure, need strategic solutions for CBDCs to succeed.
